Song of the Moonlight Road

The Song of Waiting Under the Moon

History is not only the story of kings and battles—it is also the story of ordinary hearts. As Baekje grew powerful, trade flourished across the land. Merchants journeyed with ox-carts along dusty roads and sailed to distant shores. Yet every road meant someone left behind.

In those quiet villages, a different song rose—not of armies or rulers, but of waiting wives. One such melody became known as the Song of the Moonlight Road.

They say a woman once stood under the moonlight, waiting for her husband who had gone to the market. She worked by day, but at night her heart ached with worry and longing.

And so, she sang to the moon:

Old Lyrics (Jeongeupsa)

O moon, climb high into the night, Shine your silver light afar— Shine upon the roads he travels, Guide him safely home to me. Ah, my heart—how it trembles! Is he still lingering in the marketplace? Tell me, moon, where his footsteps go. Watch that he not stumble, not falter, Nor sink into the dark and troubled mire. Ah, my heart—how it trembles! All the goods he carries, let them be— Let him leave them, let them fall aside. Only haste, only return, my love— Before his heart grows weary, before his flame burns dim. Ah, my heart—how it trembles!
