Do Not Cross The River

Korea's Oldest Song Brought Back To Life

0:00 0:00
One of the oldest songs ever recorded in Korea came from a wife standing on the bank of a river. We don't know her name. We don't know her village. But through her voice, we know her sorrow. Imagine her there: pleading, crying, warning her husband not to cross. He does not listen. He crosses—and he drowns.

We brought Korea's oldest song, Gongmudohaga, back to life with Suno AI. The original voice is long gone, but its sorrow, beauty, and longing live on in this song.

Original Lyrics

Do not cross the river, Please, my love, please stay. (公無渡河) But you would not listen, And drifted far away. (公竟渡河) Now you are gone— Lost beneath the stream. (墮河而死) My joy has vanished; Alas, what shall I do? (當奈公何)
