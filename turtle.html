Turtle, O Turtle!

The Song That Summons The Turtle

0:00 0:00
Long ago in the land of Gaya, nine village chiefs gathered on a hill called Gujibong—“Turtle Hill.” Their people were many, but they had no ruler to unite them. So the chiefs prayed together, asking the heavens to send them a king.

They danced around the hilltop, stamping their feet and raising their voices in a chant so ancient and powerful that it echoed across the sky: “Turtle, O Turtle! Show us your head! If you will not show it, we will burn you, we will roast you, we will eat you!”

Though it may sound like a threat to modern ears, it was really a sacred plea—a call for the divine to reveal a leader. The turtle represented both the heavens and the earth, and asking it to “show its head” was a way of begging heaven to reveal the one who would guide them.

Then, the heavens answered. From the clouds descended a golden chest holding six radiant eggs. From those eggs were born six boys who would grow into wise kings. The greatest among them was Suro Kim, the first ruler of Golden-Crown Gaya, who led his people into unity and strength.

Original Chant

Turtle, O Turtle! (龜何龜何) Show us your head! (首其現也) If you will not show it, (若不現也) We will burn you, We will roast you, We will eat you! (燔灼而喫也)
